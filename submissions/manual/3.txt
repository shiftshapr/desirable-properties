The UMi Economy offers a real-world, civic use case for Meta-Layer Desirable Property #4 (Data Sovereignty), demonstrating how this principle can be implemented in active digital and physical environments that already engage communities and enterprises. Through the UMi infrastructure, DP4 moves beyond concept—it becomes a living systemthat supports resilient, interoperable, and equitable civic transformation. UMi proposes a dedicated project within Desirable Property #4: Data Sovereignty of the Meta-Layer Initiative. Many members of the UMi Network are subject matter experts whose work is deeply rooted in their specific domain—such as education, health, finance, housing, or justice. While they may not be best positioned to offer general ideas about the broader internet or web infrastructure, their reflections, comments, and testimonials—grounded in real-world experience—offer valuable insight into how the Meta-Layer can be meaningfully applied within their fields. We believe this kind of sector-specific input is not only more authentic to their expertise but also more impactful. By contributing from where they stand, UMi members can help shape Desirable Property #4 in ways that speak directly to practical application, civic relevance, and community benefit—anchoring the Meta-Layer Initiative in lived, actionable knowledge. Over time, these reflections will serve as a foundational component of how DP4 is integrated into our Civic Infrastructure Project inside the UMi Economy. Our approach is anthropological—we support organic, community-aligned growth. Therefore, our intent is not simply to contribute ideas, but to create a phygital, living feedback loop by embedding DP4 as an interactive educational and engagement tool within our Intro to Data Sovereignty Workshop Series. The project will live within the UMi Sandbox and reflect our Environment and Human-Centered Implementation Framework, which fuses: --> The 10 Universal Principles for a Conscious Attention Economy including The 11th Principle: Phygital Presence --> Data Sovereignty --> Multi-Web Infrastructure Design --> GCBPR (Gamified Community-Based Participatory Research) DP4 will also be incorporated into our Web4 Implementation Playbook, guiding how Data Sovereignty is applied through Meta-Layer technologies across our civic infrastructure developments. ⬇️ Meta-Layer Desirable Properties UMi is focused on the arrangement of these very important traits of the Meta-Layer because they will support how metadata—specifically CAE-metadata—produced by meta-communities in the UMi Economy are able to interact with the various information environments of its multi-web Above the Internet fabric. This fabric will sit on top of the existing ICT Infrastructure as a new Overweb-enhanced ICT Infrastructure. Because everything about UMi is centered around utility, and we want that utility to reflect the UMi Economy’s Environment & Human-Centered Approach to Data Sovereignty, we are placing Meta-Layer Desirable Property #4 (DP4) at the forefront and near the center of UMi’s concentric-circle design. (This will be visualized with a provided design.) While UMi embraces all of the Desirable Properties of the Meta-Layer, we emphasize a purposeful order of operations, tailored to our own design. This arrangement supports UMi’s anthropological approach to technology, allowing Desirable Properties to be placed across expanding concentric circles in ways that are most meaningful to the community. * Why? Because the UMi Economy begins with the centering of life and the environment that life experiences. * From this position, the rest of the desirable properties are built upon, in an order determined by a bias-mitigated, ethical, and regenerative socioeconomic framework. UMi’s Order of Operations: * Bias-Mitigation comes first—because before something can be ethical, it must be contextually aligned with the diverse cultures experiencing it. Bias becomes a reflective tool, used to measure diversity and support accurate ethical decision-making. * Ethical comes second—once bias is identified and mitigated, ethical frameworks can be shaped through acknowledgment of who is involved in cultivating data (i.e., measurable thoughts and actions) in the phygital Above the Internet space. * Regenerative comes third—after bias has been mitigated and ethics identified, regenerative practices ensure that Meta-Layer implementation within UMi can replicate successfully while remaining flexible, sustainable, and socially and economically relevant. (Additional Context) We envision a system that recognizes the Meta-Layer as vital to the upgrading of aging civic infrastructure—a tool to solve the siloed nature of the current internet by connecting fractured ecosystems via an Overweb Infrastructure. UMi’s ecosystem includes verified Data Exchange Zones facing ongoing information insecurities. These zones span key public and private sectors. Instead of abstract contributions, our subject matter experts will offer sector-specific reflections and testimonials through the lens of Data Sovereignty and civic infrastructure. These reflections will form a body of practical, real-world input, aligned with the values and challenges facing: * JBii – Jobs * CSii – Consumer Goods & Services * FDii – Food * EDii – Education * FNii – Finance * TLii – Transportation & Logistics * PHii – Personal Health * SJii – Social Justice * PLii – Politics & Governance * HSii – Housing * EVii – Environment * ENii – Energy Our approach allows UMi’s individual and enterprise members to submit reflective, experience-based feedback—which we will curate, then channel toward the Meta-Layer Initiative as structured commentary on how DP4 could support each sector. We will also at the same time encourage participants with deeper insights and ideas to give comment directly to the Meta-Layer Initiative. 3. What could this property enable if done right? If implemented with integrity, Desirable Property #4 could become the entry point for civic infrastructure leaders and communities to engage with all Desirable Properties of the Meta-Layer. It has the potential to catalyze: * Adoption across municipalities, sectors, and industries * Collaborative civic innovation—where ideas and technologies are not just user-tested, but community-built and verified for real-world utility * A digital governance renaissance—empowering communities to determine how their data, presence, and identityare treated within Meta-Layer environments Future innovations enabled through DP4 include: * Bridges created for each of UMi’s Insecurity Initiatives—these evidence-based pathways will ensure that CAE-metadata can move contextually and ethically in the Above the Internet environment * Data Sovereignty mobile application integration with Meta-Layer technologies—enabling both individual and enterprise members to cultivate, manage, and exchange valuable data insights By operationalizing DP4, we unlock the phygital co-creation model that the UMi Economy and Meta-Layer Initiative are jointly envisioning—bridging protocols with human-centered relevance.
