Details: Contribute an Idea: ✳ Submission Title: Navigator User Interfaces (NUI) as a Coordination Layer for a Post-Search, Post-Feed Web 🧩 Summary of the Idea This submission proposes an open standard for Navigator User Interfaces (NUIs)—interfaces that provide users with customizable, shareable navigational assistance across web applications, workflows, and learning contexts. Unlike command-line or graphical interfaces, a NUI presents users with contextually relevant options (posts, paths, cards) for navigating complex tasks, enabling collaborative sense-making, automation of workflows, and ethical transparency in system design. This interface paradigm is particularly well-suited to supporting social simulation experiments, AI-human teaming, and distributed education. A NUI is envisioned not just as a design pattern, but as a shared interface standard with a federated market of “navigator providers,” enabling pluralism in interpretation and pedagogy while retaining shared grounding in a “regular panel.” This would allow users to experience different perspectives, learning pathways, or curatorial frames without fragmenting the underlying shared context—a powerful design principle for supporting collaboration across difference. 🎯 Relevance to Desirable Properties DP1 - Federated Authentication & Accountability 🔁 Change: NUIs could benefit from a standard mechanism to link user contributions and preferences across navigators. 💬 Suggested comment: “To support collaborative navigation and workflow sharing, include standard hooks for federated attribution, trust levels, and preferences in navigator components.” DP2 - Participant Agency and Empowerment ✅ Extension: The NUI’s ability to let users craft, share, and refine paths and posts gives real agency over tools and flows. 💬 Suggested comment: “The Meta-layer should explicitly support user-authored workflow components (e.g., posts/paths), enabling granular agency in tool construction.” DP4 - Data Sovereignty and Privacy 🔁 Clarification: Sharing posts and paths raises privacy questions, especially for educational or organizational use. 💬 Suggested comment: “Clarify how shared navigational flows (and subscriptions) would respect user privacy and support fine-grained control over visibility and attribution.” DP5 - Decentralized Namespace ✅ Extension: The NUI design suggests paths and posts be shareable and searchable across networks. 💬 Suggested comment: “Support a decentralized namespace for posts, paths, and navigational elements, enabling remix and reuse across different platforms and contexts.” DP7 - Simplicity and Interoperability ✅ Extension: NUIs could function as a browser overlay or cross-site sidebar, a unifying layer for diverse platforms. 💬 Suggested comment: “The Meta-layer should support modular navigator frameworks that can plug into any web context—much like browser extensions or OS-level sidebars.” DP8 - Collaborative Environment and Meta-Communities ✅ Extension: NUIs explicitly foster community curation and shared workflow layers—essential for knowledge networks. 💬 Suggested comment: “Highlight NUIs as tools for building collaborative meta-environments across diverse communities, enabling remixable pedagogical and research flows.” DP10 - Education ✅ Extension: The NUI design includes detailed support for teacher/student workflows, adaptive feedback, and comprehension checks. 💬 Suggested comment: “Encourage integration of NUIs as scaffolds for open-ended learning and live pedagogy, especially in decentralized or adult learning contexts.” DP11 - Safe and Ethical AI & DP12 - Community-based AI Governance ✅ Extension: The design supports user-directed AI agents within workflows, with feedback loops and comparative evaluation. 💬 Suggested comment: “Use NUI paths to formalize and surface AI behaviors, enabling transparent governance of AI contributions.” DP14 - Trust and Transparency ✅ Extension: The NUI's layered view (white-box approximations, versioning, edit history) advances explainability. 💬 Suggested comment: “Build tools for transparent and versioned exploration of shared navigational flows—who edited what, when, and why.” DP18 - Feedback Loops and Reputation ✅ Extension: NUIs track user interaction, which can be used to inform curation, preference propagation, and reputation. 💬 Suggested comment: “Explore how usage of posts and paths across networks can fuel reputation systems and distributed evaluation.” DP20 - Community Ownership ✅ Extension: By allowing users to construct and curate their own navigational experience, NUIs empower shared ownership of knowledge flows. 💬 Suggested comment: “Position navigators as community-curated infrastructure, not just UX patterns.”
