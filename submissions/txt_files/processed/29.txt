Suggested extension: Public grounding entails not only commons-aligned interface infrastructure but also openly steered meta-institutions, accountable to the collective body of contributors. 🆕 Proposed DP23: Living Governance of the Meta-Layer Initiative Title: DP23: Living Governance of the Meta-Layer Initiative Draft Wording: The Meta-Layer Initiative must embody the values and regenerative patterns it aims to encode. This includes governing itself as a living, evolving commons—transparent, participatory, consent-driven, and purpose-aligned. Governance should be rooted in visible rituals, reflexive documentation, rotating stewardship, and meaningful dissent pathways. Like the infrastructures it aims to catalyze, its legitimacy arises from its capacity to learn, adapt, and cultivate belonging through process. ✨ Core Elements of DP23: Governance-as-practice: Treat meta-governance as a visible, iterable protocol space (e.g., Meta-RFCs, pattern evaluations). Consent over control: Emphasize reflexive discernment and opposition mapping over top-down decision-making. Rotating stewardship: Role renewal modeled after NomCom or sortition to distribute insight and avoid capture. Pattern coherence: Use pattern languages (e.g., Group Works, Wise Democracy) to evaluate and evolve project culture and coordination. 📘 Suggested Annotation for DP23: Inspired by regenerative precedents like the IETF, this property frames the Meta-Layer Initiative as itself a commons process—one whose governance must remain as open, reflective, and alive as the civic substrates it aims to enable.
