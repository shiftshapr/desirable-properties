Contribute an Idea: Seeding Generational Familiarity with the Meta-Layer Through Purpose-Driven Educational Use and Scandinavian Journalism Partnerships Contribution Overview This submission proposes a dual-entry approach to introduce the Meta-Layer and the Overweb to broad audiences by focusing on two interrelated use cases: education and journalism. First, in the school sector—particularly across Europe—project-based learning environments can integrate Meta-Layer tools to foster youth engagement with a safer, purpose-driven, and participatory web. Students working on global goals, cultural heritage, and civic development would gain multi-year familiarity with bridge tools, truth annotation, and collaborative knowledge-making. Second, Scandinavia represents a particularly fertile region for a journalist-specific integration of the Meta-Layer. With strong traditions of collaborative civic culture, peaceful education systems, and relatively depolarized journalism ecosystems, countries like Denmark and Sweden could pioneer the use of a dedicated journalistic layer. This would empower newsrooms to verify content, trace sources, and resist the creep of misinformation. The educational and journalistic use cases are mutually reinforcing and could together provide a resilient first foothold for broader adoption. Clarifications & Extensions DP10 – Education: Integrative Use in Formal Curricula Extension: This submission proposes the use of the Meta-Layer not just as a supplementary digital tool but as an integral component of school curricula through cross-disciplinary projects tied to sustainability, citizenship, and intercultural dialogue. Educators would use Meta-Layer tools to frame student inquiries, research collaborations, and public project presentations. This educational use-case includes youth-led bridge-building, annotation, and participation in SDG-aligned initiatives. Why it matters: By becoming a living part of students' education, the Meta-Layer becomes not merely a tool but an environment for formative learning. This creates a generation fluent in meta-contextual navigation, digital ethics, and collaborative sense-making—crucial skills for democratic resilience and sustainable futures. DP14 – Trust and Transparency: Scandinavian Journalism as a Pilot Region Extension: This submission highlights Scandinavian newsrooms as ideal candidates for early implementation of a journalist-specific layer within the Meta-Layer. Leveraging regional strengths in civic trust, low polarization, and collaborative journalism cultures, such a pilot could validate tools for truth-marking, traceable sourcing, and cross-publication bridge-building. Why it matters: Unlike regions already saturated with toxic misinformation, Scandinavia still maintains relatively healthy information ecosystems. This makes it feasible to test and demonstrate how the Meta-Layer can strengthen journalism’s role in preserving public trust—while offering replicable models for other regions. (End of Submission)
