Contribute an Idea: Based on Dr. Paul John Werbos's Saving the World and New Jerusalem posts, here is a breakdown of the Desirable Properties requiring clarification or extension, formatted as you requested: DP1: Federated Authentication & Accountability - Extending Accountability to Non-Human Agents Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: This property states that participants should be "accountable for their actions". The sources introduce advanced AI entities, specifically AGI, which can be embodied in mammal brains, sophisticated silicon substrates, or hybrids. These entities have an output or action vector. The "New Jerusalem" concept involves complex physically embodied systems like global sensor networks and potentially space habitats, which may be managed by or interact with advanced AI. Clarification is needed on how accountability is established, tracked, and enforced for these advanced AI agents and physically embodied systems, not just human participants. How is an AGI or complex system authenticated or identified in a federated system? What constitutes an "action" by a complex system or an AI with an action vector, and how is accountability assigned, especially when actions are autonomous or part of larger system behaviors? Why it matters: The sources highlight the desperate need for new integration packages and networks of communication between "key players and systems" to limit conflicts and chaos, implicitly demanding accountability frameworks that extend beyond human users to these non-human entities and systems. Constructing systems based on deep knowledge of intelligent systems is seen as necessary to reduce the need for crude regulation. DP2: Participant Agency and Empowerment - Defining Agency for AI and Systems Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: DP2 states that participants should "control their presence" and implies empowerment. The sources define an intelligent system (AGI) as having an input vector, an output or action vector, and an intrinsic value scalar. AGI is described as capable of creativity depending on its intelligence level and embodiment. Human-agent interaction is anticipated in the Meta-Layer. This DP requires clarification on what "agency" means for advanced AI entities and embodied systems. Do they "control their presence"? What constitutes "empowerment" for a non-human agent with an action vector? How does the Meta-Layer framework distinguish between human agency and the agency of an AGI or a complex automated system, especially one managing critical infrastructure? Why it matters: The mathematical definition of an intelligent system having an "action vector u" directly points to AI agency, necessitating an extension of this property to define the scope and nature of this agency within the Meta-Layer framework and how it interacts with human participants. DP3: Adaptive Governance Supporting an Exponentially Growing Community - Including System Complexity in Governance Nature: Extension. Description: DP3 focuses on governance adapting to "community growth". While this includes human participants, the sources describe a future involving highly capable AI managing parts of the internet or critical infrastructure. The sources also mention the urgency of building new connections between "key players and systems" to address risks from AI/IoT and limit crude regulation by constructing systems based on deep knowledge of intelligent systems. This DP needs extension to explicitly include the challenge of governing complex systems and advanced, potentially non-human agents that may manage critical infrastructure. Why it matters: Governance must adapt not just to the number of human users but to the types of entities and systems integrated, their capabilities, and the complex interactions they enable, particularly those with the potential for existential risk. DP4: Data Sovereignty and Privacy & DP5: Decentralized Namespace - Applying Digital Rights to Non-Human Entities Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: DP4 states that participants must "fully own their data, identity, and digital space", while DP5 mentions persistent namespaces anchored in "personal control". The sources introduce the concept of AGI and embodied systems. These entities will likely generate and process vast amounts of data and may require some form of digital identity or namespace. These DPs require clarification on how "data sovereignty," "ownership," "identity," and "personal control" apply to non-human agents and systems. Can an AGI "own" data it generates or processes? Does an embodied sensor network have a "digital space" or an "identity" in the same sense as a human? The current definition appears human-centric ("human-centered identity", "personal control"), necessitating an extension to define how these concepts apply to or are managed in relation to non-human entities within the Meta-Layer framework. Why it matters: As the Meta-Layer incorporates advanced AI and systems, establishing clear principles for their data, identity, and digital presence is crucial for security, privacy, and interoperability, extending these fundamental rights or concepts beyond human users. DP6: Commerce - Non-Human Participation in Value Exchange Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: DP6 pertains to commerce within the Meta-Layer, expected to unlock interactions that are "an opportunity for exchange or creation of value". Given the introduction of AGI capable of actions and potentially managing infrastructure, clarification is needed on whether these non-human entities are envisioned as participants in commerce or value exchange. Can an AGI create or exchange value? Can it own or manage assets involved in commerce? Why it matters: While the sources don't explicitly detail AI participation in commerce, the potential for advanced AI to perform tasks and manage systems that are inherently linked to economic activity implies this DP may require extension beyond human participants to fully define the potential economic landscape of the Meta-Layer. DP7: Simplicity and Interoperability, DP8: Collaborative Environment and Meta-Communities, & DP9: Developer and Community Incentives - Including AI in Collaboration and Community Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: These DPs emphasize enabling fluid movement across domains, amplifying collaborative potential, and providing incentives within a collaborative environment and meta-communities. The sources explicitly mention "human-agent interaction" as part of the Meta-Layer vision and highlight the need for new networks of communication to build connections between "key players and systems". This implies that advanced AI agents and systems are expected to be "participants" in some capacity. Clarification is needed on how the concepts of "collaboration," "community," and "participant" apply to non-human entities. Can an AGI "collaborate" or be part of a "meta-community"? How does interoperability function between human and non-human agents/systems? Are non-human agents eligible for "incentives" or involved in the "developer" process? Why it matters: The existing descriptions of community and participation seem centered around human contributors and co-creators, requiring extension to explicitly define the roles and integration of non-human agents in the Meta-Layer's collaborative environment. DP11: Safe and Ethical AI, DP12: Community-Based AI Governance, & DP13: AI Containment - Specifying Governance for Advanced, Embodied AI Nature: Clarification and Specification. Description: These DPs are explicitly dedicated to AI Governance & Safety, requiring AI to operate within ethical/safety frameworks and be contained and governed by the community. The sources reinforce the critical importance and urgency of these concerns, highlighting the potential for existential risk from AI/IoT and discussing AGI embodied in various forms (silicon, mammal, hybrid) and potentially managing global sensor networks and critical infrastructure. These DPs require clarification and specification regarding how safety, ethics, governance, and containment mechanisms will function for the specific types of advanced, potentially embodied, and infrastructure-managing AI discussed in the sources. How can an AGI managing critical systems like defense technologies or global sensor networks be effectively contained? What specific ethical frameworks are applicable to AI embodied in silicon or hybrid forms? Community-based governance needs definition in the context of entities managing complex physical systems. Why it matters: The sources stress the existential threats posed by uncontrolled AI/IoT development, making the clear definition and implementation of these safety and governance DPs paramount, especially for the powerful AI systems envisioned. DP14: Trust and Transparency & DP15: Security and Provenance - Ensuring Trust and Security with Non-Human Agents and Systems Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: These DPs emphasize building trust through secure infrastructure, transparent decision-making, auditability, verifiability, and security with provenance. The sources introduce advanced AI entities and complex systems, some operating in sensitive defense and space contexts as part of the "New Jerusalem" technologies, such as sophisticated quantum information processing for surveillance or intelligent guidance systems. Clarification is needed on how trust and transparency are established and maintained in interactions with or involving non-human agents and complex systems. How can the actions and decisions of an AGI be made transparent and auditable? How is the provenance of actions initiated or mediated by AI or automated systems tracked and verified? Why it matters: The security implications are significantly heightened by the presence of powerful AI and complex infrastructure, especially given concerns about cyber capabilities and risks mentioned in the sources. A clear definition of how security, trust, and provenance are ensured across human and non-human participants and system components is essential. DP18: Feedback Loops and Reputation, DP19: Amplifying Presence and Community Engagement, & DP20: Community Ownership - Community Participation by Non-Human Entities Nature: Clarification and Extension. Description: These DPs describe a community-evolving system where contributors become co-creators through feedback, engagement, and ownership. The Meta-Layer involves "human-agent interaction". These properties require clarification on the extent to which non-human agents (like AGI) can participate in these processes. Can an AGI provide "feedback"? Can it have a "reputation" within the system? What does "engagement" mean for an automated system? Can non-human entities participate in "community ownership"? Why it matters: The current framing of community and participation is largely human-centric, and needs to be extended to explicitly define if and how non-human entities fit into this model, given their anticipated presence and interaction within the Meta-Layer.
