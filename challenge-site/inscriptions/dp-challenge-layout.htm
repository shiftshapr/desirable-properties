The Desirable Properties Challenge

Define the Foundations of a Trustworthy Meta-Layer

The Internet was built for pages—not for synthetic media, AI agents, contextual trust, or collective governance. The Meta-Layer Initiative is now focused on the Desirable Properties Challenge: a global, community-driven effort to define what must be true of a civic layer above the webpage.

Desirable Properties are not technical specifications. They are design principles for trustworthy digital environments—conditions that support agency, consent, transparency, accountability, safety, participation, and collective intelligence.

Twenty-one draft properties are maintained on govhub.live. Living drafts evolve through workgroups, comments, and patches. This satplication anchors the canonical on-chain corpus: each property, the framing chapter, and companion essays inscribed as enduring digital artifacts on Bitcoin.

Explore the properties, read the drafts, and propose patches on Gov Hub. Press i while viewing any article for inscription metadata. Visit desirableproperties.org for the public challenge home.
