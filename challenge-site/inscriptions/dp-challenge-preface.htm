The Desirable Properties Challenge

A Digital Monument to Collective Design

You are inside a permanent digital monument. Inscribed on Bitcoin, it preserves the full provenance of the Desirable Properties—twenty-two design principles for a trustworthy civic layer above the webpage.

What you'll find here:

  • 31 early PCI conversations (Sep–Nov 2023) — the founding emails from the People Centered Internet community that seeded the vision
  • 46 Second Call for Input submissions (2024) — proposals from researchers, builders, and communities around the world
  • 22 Desirable Property drafts — living documents stewarded on Gov Hub, each one a candidate design principle for the Meta-Layer

These drafts are the product of many streams: The Metaweb book, two public calls for input, the Meta-Layer Initiative's ongoing research, the Canopi implementation, online meetings and workshops, and countless conversations across continents and disciplines.

ChatGPT was used as a synthesizing agent—reading every submission, mapping alignments, and drafting initial text. But synthesis is not governance. These versions are proposals, not conclusions.

They are waiting for you.

The near-term goal of the Desirable Properties Challenge is straightforward: get real human eyes, minds, and voices on every draft. Read them. Critique them. Propose patches. Approve what rings true. Reject what doesn't. Shape the principles that will define how trust, identity, privacy, AI safety, and collective intelligence work in the next layer of the Internet.

Every contribution is preserved. Every voice matters. The Challenge runs on desirableproperties.org and workgroups live on govhub.live.
